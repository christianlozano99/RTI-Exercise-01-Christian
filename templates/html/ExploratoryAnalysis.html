Income and Work Analysis¶

Here I'll be looking at cross table graphs to try and visulize some of the correlations mentioned earlier, to see if there is any meaningful trends.

Occupation vs Income¶

Here we can observe that the two occupations with the highest pay are either professional specialties and executive mangerial workers. This most likely correlates with the education and experience required to be in either position.

In [43]:
crossTable = pd.crosstab(catagorical_attributes['occupations'], catagorical_attributes['over_50k']).plot.bar(stacked = False, figsize = (12,7)).set(title = "Occupation vs Income")

Education vs Income¶

Now looking at education aginst income it further proves that more pay comes with education. We can see here that as education is higher the proportion of under 50k to over 50k begins to lean more towards more people earning over 50k.

In [45]:

Age vs Income¶

Here we can also observe that when it comes to younger people and older people there is a less likelyhood of making over 50k per year. This is probably due to either low experience or retiring. So the age group that earns the most seems to come from the 40 to 50 year olds. Which makes sense since they are usually the most experienced.

Here we can observe that after passing the 40 hour mark, or being full time, leads to a bigger proprtion of having an income over 50k per year compared to making less lower. With the best spot being between 40-50 hours per week.

Summary¶

From data exploration there is quite a bit of conclusions that we would be able to draw about the population in this time period.

  • We were able to see that with age, experience/education, and hours worked per week, all correlated to making an income over 50k per year. Although it seems like the most influencial factor is edcucation.

  • We were also able to see possibly why men had a higher proportion of making over 50k than women. With the most influenctial factor seeming to be that more men worked more hours a week than women.
